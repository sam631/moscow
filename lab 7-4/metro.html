The Moscow Metro

The Moscow metro was constructed in the early 1930s and is famous for its palatial marble stations with mosaics, chandeliers, and precious materials. It is without a doubt the most beautiful in the world. Moscow's metro stations are a heritage of the socialist regime, otginally intended to be "palaces for the people". The construction of the Moscow Metro was begun in the early 1930s and is world famous for its palatial marble stations with mosaics, chandeliers and precious materials. The first line opened May 1935 and two more lines were added later.

There is an old story as to how the ring line for the Moscow Metro was concieved. A group of architects came to Joseph Stalin with the merto blueprints to let him know about the progress and what was being done at the moment. While looking at the drawings, Stalin poured himself some coffee and some coffee dripped onto the side of his cup. When he was asked whether he liked the project so far or not, he put his cup in the center of the Metro blueprints, and left in silence. The bottom of the cup left a brown cirlce on the drawings. The architects looked at them and realized that it was exactly what they needed. They took it as a sign of Stalin's genius and rushed to the construction site to give orders for the building ring line. This legend, of course, may be attributed to Stalin's cult of personallity. However, if you look at the map of the Moscow Metro, the ring line is always printed in brown on the maps.

The Metro had endured and survived more test than just the constant grind of people everyday. During World War II, the Metro was used as a shelter. It being underground, withstood the war and protected those encased within the walls. Some of the stations are five stories deep, hence showimg their security as a bomb shelter. They were also designated as shelters during the Cold War in case Nuclear War broke out between the Soviet Union and the United States.

One mystery often mentioned is the so called "Metro-2". It is thought that the particular Moscow metro contains a secret tunnel network. It connects the Kremiln, Minstry of Defense, bunkers, command posts, and other strategic objects. These tunnels run for tens of kilometers (and far out of Moscow!). They would allow quick evacuation of Russian leadership should nuclear war break out. They lie much deeper than usual metro system. It seems hard to believe, but here is another fact "Metro-2" is not the only secret object in Moscow. There is a whole underground city built by the military during the Cold War.
